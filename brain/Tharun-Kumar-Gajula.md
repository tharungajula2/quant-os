# Tharun Kumar Gajula

I work around the intersection of credit risk, quantitative analytics, model validation, and implementation.

My background is not purely academic and not purely technical. It comes from working close to banking and lending workflows, understanding how risk is measured and monitored, and then building a stronger quantitative and model-focused layer on top of that.

This vault is how I organize that work.

It is my working system for understanding how risk models are built, challenged, monitored, and used inside real institutions.

## What this system is for

The main purpose of this vault is to connect five things properly:

- credit risk fundamentals
- regulatory frameworks
- model development
- model validation and monitoring
- practical implementation

I did not want separate notes on Basel, IFRS 9, PD, scorecards, validation, and ML sitting in isolation. The point of the system is to make them connect.

## How this connects to my work

At Jana Small Finance Bank, I worked close to credit risk analytics, portfolio monitoring, reporting, and PD/EAD/LGD-style thinking. That gave me practical exposure to how risk is tracked inside a bank and how reporting, controls, and portfolio behaviour matter beyond just model outputs.

At Lentra AI, I worked closer to the lending-system side. That included workflows, business requirements, UAT, API validation, and implementation logic. That experience matters because models do not live in isolation. They sit inside systems, processes, and decision flows.

Together, these two parts shaped the way I think about risk work:
not just as analysis, and not just as business process, but as a combination of model logic, governance, data, and implementation.
